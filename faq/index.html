Frequently Asked Questions

Can I bring my kids?

Yes. This event is for all ages - though high school and up would probably get the most out of it.

Will materials be provided?

Possibly - we are working with local maker groups on this - stay tuned!

Are projects supposed to be started already?

Ideally, though there will be some hands-on tutorials for those who want to start something from scratch.

Do I need a ticket?

Absolutely not. You’re free to come and go as you please. However, we encourage you to register in advance to help us estimate attendance.

Is there a floor plan for Tec^Edge?

Yes, see here

What room will presentations be made in?

The presentations will be in the Einstein Room with screencast / spillover into the Founders Room. See map

What kinds of connectors and gear do we need for presentations?

There are VGA and HDMI cables as well as standard 3.5mm audio mini jacks for the A/V system. Plan on bringing your own adapters (e.g. for MacBook).

Is food provided?

Food will be available for purchase from the following local food trucks: Lilia’s Outside Cafe, Pa’s Pork, Just Jerk

What booth space is available to exhibitors? Is it free?

Booth space is free, and currently there is no shortage. Just let us know via the this page how much you need.

What is a maker fest?

We are leaving this very open ended. It could be anything from hobby electronics to 3D printing to arts and crafts. See Instructables.com for some good examples. Basically, we want you to show us anything cool you have made in your free time. Because it's International Pi Day, expect to see a lot of Raspberry Pi projects, though Arduino and BeagleBone are certainly welcome too!

To quote Wikipedia

Typical interests enjoyed by the maker culture include engineering-oriented pursuits such as electronics, robotics, 3-D printing, and the use of CNC tools, as well as more traditional activities such as metalworking, woodworking, and traditional arts and crafts. The subculture stresses a cut-and-paste approach to standardized hobbyist technologies, and encourages cookbook re-use of designs published on websites and maker-oriented publications. Learning emphasizes learning-through-doing (constructivism) in a social environment. Maker culture emphasizes informal, networked, peer-led, and shared learning motivated by fun and self-fulfillment.
